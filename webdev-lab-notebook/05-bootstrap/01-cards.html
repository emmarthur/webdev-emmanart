Chess

Chess is one of the world’s oldest board games. Each player starts with sixteen pieces and takes turns moving according to fixed rules. The goal is to place the opponent’s king in an inescapable attack—checkmate.

Great for learning planning, pattern recognition, and thinking several moves ahead.

Sudoku

Sudoku puzzles start with a partially filled grid. Using logic alone—no guessing required in well-formed puzzles—you deduce where each digit belongs until the grid is complete.

Difficulty scales from easy beginner grids to expert puzzles with few starting clues.

Tetris

Tetrominoes fall one at a time; you move and rotate them to form solid rows. When a row is complete it clears, scoring points and freeing space. Speed increases as you progress.

The game rewards quick decisions and spatial awareness under pressure.
